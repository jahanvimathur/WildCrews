Ranthambore National Park offers Jeep safaris and canter (open bus) safaris. Jeep safaris are more private and flexible, accommodating around 4-6 people and lasting about 3-4 hours. Canter safaris are shared and accommodate more passengers, lasting around 3 hours. Both safaris are conducted during early morning and late afternoon, providing ample opportunities for wildlife viewing.

Ranthambore National Park has around 10 safari zones. Each zone offers distinct ecosystems and wildlife encounters, contributing to a varied and exciting safari experience. Please verify the current zone count before planning your visit.

Safari costs in Ranthambore vary based on factors like vehicle type, zone, and nationality. On average, prices can range from $20 to $100 per person for Indians and from $60 to $150 for foreign nationals. The fee generally includes the park entry, guide charges, vehicle cost, and government taxes. It's advisable to confirm the specific inclusions with your safari provider to ensure a clear understanding of the cost breakdown.

Consider visiting Ranthambore during the dry season (October to April) for optimal wildlife sightings as animals congregate around water sources. The months of October to December offer pleasant weather and fewer crowds. The park is closed during the monsoon season (July to September). Planning around local festivals or bird migration seasons can also add unique experiences to your visit.
