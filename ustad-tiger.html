Introduction

Ustad (T-24)

Ustad, also known as T-24 or "Scarface," was a dominant male tiger in Ranthambore National Park, Rajasthan, India. He gained significant notoriety and attention both for his striking appearance and for the controversies and debates surrounding his management and eventual relocation.Ustad was named "Scarface" due to a prominent and distinctive scar on his face, which only added to his unique and formidable presence in the park. His striking appearance made him a favorite subject for wildlife photographers and enthusiasts who visited Ranthambore.

However, Ustad's fame was not without controversy. He was involved in a few incidents of human-tiger conflict, including the death of a forest guard in 2015. These incidents led to concerns about the safety of both park staff and visitors. As a result, there were debates and discussions about whether Ustad should be relocated to a more remote area or confined in an enclosure to minimize potential conflicts.

In May 2015, Ustad was controversially relocated to the Sajjangarh Biological Park in Udaipur, away from his familiar territory in Ranthambore. This decision sparked widespread debates and protests, with some arguing that Ustad should have been allowed to remain in his natural habitat. Supporters of his relocation cited concerns about human safety and the need to avoid further incidents.While in captivity at Sajjangarh Biological Park, Ustad's life became less visible to the public, and he was kept in a controlled environment. His situation drew attention from animal rights activists and organizations who advocated for his return to the wild.

Ustad's story highlighted the complex and challenging issues involved in managing human-tiger interactions and balancing the interests of both conservation and safety. His case also raised awareness about the importance of addressing such conflicts in a way that ensures the well-being of both tigers and the local communities that share their habitat.
